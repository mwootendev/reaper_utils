# **GLOBAL CONCEPTS (Quick Primer)**

# ------------------------------------------------------------

This section ensures the rest of the manual uses consistent terminology.

---

## ğŸš **Equalization (EQ)**

Boosting or cutting frequency bands to shape tonal balance.

You adjust EQ to:

* Remove muddiness (low-mid cuts)
* Add brightness (high-shelf boosts)
* Carve space between instruments (narrow cuts)
* Correct resonance problems
* Sculpt tone (broad shelves)

---

## ğŸ§° **Compression**

Reduces dynamic range to make audio more consistent and punchy.

Used for:

* Bringing vocals forward
* Tightening bass
* Punching up drums
* Evening out guitars
* Adding aggression or smoothness

Parameters include: threshold, ratio, attack, release, knee, makeup gain.

---

## ğŸ”‰ **Limiting**

Very fast, high-ratio compression used to:

* Prevent peaks
* Raise overall loudness
* Protect the master bus

A limiter is typically the last stage of a mastering chain.

* Clipping

Used on vocals, bass, drums, guitars, and whole mixes for thickness.

## ğŸŒŠ **Reverb**
